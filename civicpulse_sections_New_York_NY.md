New York saw major transit and street-policy moves as the City Council backed fully subsidized Fair Fares for 1.3 million riders while state and local leaders pushed to curb highway expansion, improve air quality, and reform trash and streetscape management. Funding gaps and governance strains also stood out, from unfunded containerization and volunteer fire companies to a federal raid on a City Council member and efforts to avert health insurance losses for 450,000 residents.
